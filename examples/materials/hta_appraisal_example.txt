HTA Appraisal Summary

Decision context: reimbursement decision for first-line treatment in advanced condition X.
Target population: adults with confirmed diagnosis of condition X after biomarker testing.
Comparators: standard chemotherapy regimen A and targeted therapy B.
Perspective: national healthcare payer perspective.
Time horizon: lifetime horizon is preferred to capture all survival and cost differences.
Cycle length: monthly cycle selected because treatment assessments occur every 4 weeks.
Model structure: state-transition Markov model with progression-free, progressed disease, and death states.
Clinical inputs: overall survival and progression-free survival from randomized controlled trial data.
Cost inputs: drug acquisition, administration, adverse event management, follow-up, and terminal care.
Utility inputs: EQ-5D utility estimates with disutilities for grade 3+ adverse events.
Uncertainty: deterministic one-way sensitivity analyses and probabilistic sensitivity analysis required.
Known gap: immature long-term survival data, requiring extrapolation assumptions.
